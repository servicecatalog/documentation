General

What is a marketplace?

A marketplace offers services to which you can subscribe and which you can use afterwards. Subscribing to a service means that you accept the terms and conditions of using the marketplace and the license and charging conditions of the service.

The marketplace owner can decide which of the following features are available on the marketplace:

  • Tag cloud
  • Ratings and reviews
  • Categories
  • Social bookmarks

What is a service?

A service is a software you can access and use over the Web (the marketplace). As soon as you have subscribed to a service and are assigned to it, you can use it according to the conditions which must be accepted when subscribing to the service.

Who can do what on a marketplace?

Every visitor of a marketplace can:

  • Browse the service catalog, search for services, and view the service descriptions.
  • Sort the services by different criteria, for example, in alphabetical order.
  • Filter the services by terms of the tag cloud or by categories.
  • Bookmark and share services in social networks.

If you are registered and logged in, you can:

  • Use the services you have subscribed to.
  • Report issues on services you have subscribed to.
  • Rate services as well as write comments on services you have subscribed to.
  • Administrate your profile.

If you have administrator rights, you can:

  • Subscribe to services and manage all subscriptions created by you or other users in your organization.
  • Administrate users, payment information, and processes.
  • Create various reports on the services.

If you were given the role of a subscription manager, you can:

  • Subscribe to services and manage your own subscriptions.

If you were given the role of an OU administrator, you can:

  • Manage the organizational units (OU) for which you are an administrator.
  • Subscribe to services and manage the subscriptions for your organizational units.

How can I restrict the access to services?

You can restrict the access to services on a marketplace by means of organizational units.

An organizational unit is a set of one or more users representing, for example, a department in a company, an individual project, or a single person. As an organization administrator, you can create and manage organizational units and assign OU administrators to them (click here for details). You can define which marketable services are visible to the members of a unit.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Users & Units on the left side.
  4. On the Organizational units tab, click Add Unit to define a new unit, or click to edit an existing unit.
  5. Check the services that are to be visible to the members of the unit.

OU administrators can further restrict the list of services made available to their organizational units by the organization administrator. Here is how:

  1. Log in as an OU administrator.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Organizational units on the left side.
  4. In the Organizational units table, click to edit an existing unit.
  5. Check the services that are to be visible to the members of the unit.

Be aware of the following:

As an organization administrator, OU administrator, or subscription manager, you can upgrade or downgrade subscriptions. If you do so, make sure that all services that are involved in an up/downgrade path are accessible in the same organizational unit. The available services are defined by the supplier.

I have forgotten my login data, how do I retrieve it?

If you have forgotten your password, click Login in the top bar or Get it now when viewing a service. In the login dialog that shows up, click Forgot your password?. This allows you to define a new password for your user ID.

If you have forgotten your user ID, contact your administrator and tell him your email address or first and last name. The administrator can look up your user ID.

I encounter problems when using the marketplace, what may be the reason?

The reason for the problems may be your browser settings. You must enable cookies. Usually, cookies are enabled by default.

Registration

Why do I need to register?

You can only subscribe to services when you are registered. By registering, you accept the terms and conditions of using the marketplace.

You can either register yourself or you can be registered by the organization which offers a service you want to subscribe to.

As an organization administrator, you can register other users and assign them to subscriptions.

How do I register?

You need to register before you can log in.

Here is how:

  1. Either click Login in the top bar, or click Get it now when viewing a service. In both cases, a login dialog shows up.
  2. Click Register, and enter the data required for creating your account. Fields marked with an asterisk (*) are mandatory. If the Register button is not available, contact the seller of a service you want to subscribe to and ask for a registration.
  3. To confirm your registration, click Register.

You are sent an email with the URL by which you need to confirm the registration. Afterwards, you can log in with the user ID and password you chose.

Search

How do I search for services?

You can search for terms and phrases in the name, short description, and description of a service. Additionally, you can search for services by the categories assigned to them.

Here is how:

  1. Enter the search term in the search field in the top bar.
  2. Click .

The services matching the search criteria are listed.

Why can't I find the service I am looking for?

The reason may be that searchable content exists in different languages. The default language is English. You can search for terms and phrases either in the language you have selected or in the default language. If you combine search terms of the default language and your selected language in the same search phrase, the service cannot be found. This means, that you should always use search terms of one language only.

Example: The English name of a service is "Highlander", there is no specific German name. The short description, "Kurzbeschreibung", only exists in German, there is no English short description. If you search for the service with your German profile using the search phrase "Highlander Kurzbeschreibung", the service will not be found. If you use "Highlander" or "Kurzbeschreibung" as the search phrase, it will be found.

The reason may also be the organizational unit to which you belong. It may have limited access to services. Contact your administrator if you can't find the service you are looking for.

Subscription

How do I subscribe to a service?

You can subscribe to a service, if you are logged in as an organization administrator, OU administrator, or subscription manager.

Here is how:

  1. Select the service to which you want to subscribe, or click Subscribe.
  2. Click Get it now.

    If you are not yet logged in, enter your login data.

    If you are not yet registered, click Register and register first. In case there is no Register button, contact the seller of the service and ask for a registration.
  3. On the Configuration page, define the subscription details.
  4. If defined for the service, choose from different options in the Parameters and options section. The prices for the options are listed. If the Configure button is available, you can access an external tool for configuring the parameters.
  5. If you are subscribing to a service that is not free of charge, you can display details on the charging conditions and prices on the Price model tab.

    On the Payment page, select your preferred payment type and billing address. As a user with administrative rights, you can also enter a new billing address.
  6. Read and accept the license agreement, and confirm your subscription.
  7. To assign yourself or other users to the subscription, click Assign users. You may have been assigned automatically to the subscription, depending on the definition of the service you are subscribing to.

How do I assign users to a subscription?

Users must be assigned to a subscription before they can use the underlying software.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator, or as a subscription manager or OU administrator responsible for the subscription.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Subscriptions on the left side. As an organization administrator, you can see and manage all subscriptions of your organization. As a subscription manager, you can access your own subscriptions. As an OU administrator, you can access your own subscriptions and those of your organizational units.
  4. Click for the subscription to which you want to assign users.
  5. Click Assign Users.
  6. In the list of users, assign users by selecting the corresponding check boxes.
  7. If the subscription's service defines roles, select the required role for each user in the Service role column. The service roles are mapped to specific permissions in the underlying software.
  8. To save your settings, click Assign. The assigned users will be informed by email about how to access the service.

Note of the following:

You can assign any user to a subscription, regardless of the organizational units the user or the subscription belong to.

How do I use a service I have subscribed to?

As soon as you are assigned to a subscription, you can use the underlying service.

Either access the service by the link you received with an email or proceed with the following steps:

  1. Log in.
  2. Click My Subscriptions in the top bar.
  3. Find the subscription you want to use.
  4. In the Details section, click the link or Use Service.

The marketplace can also provide a Use Service button for the subscription directly on the marketplace home page. If available, click Use Service to instantly use the service.

How do I report an issue on a service?

You can report any issues you encounter in using a service to the seller of the service.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator, or as a subscription manager or OU administrator responsible for the subscription.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Subscriptions on the left side. As an organization administrator, you can see and manage all subscriptions of your organization. As a subscription manager, you can access your own subscriptions. As an OU administrator, you can access your own subscriptions and those of your organizational units.
  4. Click for the subscription for which you want to report an issue.
  5. Click the Supplier or Reseller tab.
  6. Click Report Issue.
  7. Enter a subject for the issue and give details on the question, problem, or incident you want to report.

The customer support of the seller offering the service is instantly sent an email with the information you provided.

How do I write a review on a service?

If you have already subscribed to a service, you can rate it and write a review comment. Other marketplace visitors can see your ratings and comments.

Here is how:

  1. In the service catalog, click the service.
  2. Click Show Details.
  3. Click Write a Review.
  4. Click a star to rate the service, enter a title, and type your comment.
  5. Click Save.

The rating and review feature may be switched off by the marketplace owner. In this case, you cannot rate services or write comments on them.

What is the Execute button of a subscription and how do I use it?

Services may support special operations, such as backup, shutdown, or startup of a specific application, depending on the implementation of the underlying software. If defined for a service, you can carry out such operations from a subscription.

Here is how:

  1. Click My Subscriptions in the top bar.
  2. Find the subscription you want to use.
  3. In the Details section, from the subscription's drop-down list, select the operation you want to execute. A detailed explanation of the operation is displayed.
  4. Click Execute.

Depending on how the operation is defined in the underlying service, the result of the operation is displayed instantly after its execution, or the status of execution is displayed (for asynchronous, long-running operations).

How do I deassign users from a subscription?

If you want to prevent the access of certain users to a subscription, you must deassign these users from the subscription.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator, or as a subscription manager or OU administrator responsible for the subscription.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Subscriptions on the left side. As an organization administrator, you can see and manage all subscriptions of your organization. As a subscription manager, you can access your own subscriptions. As an OU administrator, you can access your own subscriptions and those of your organizational units.
  4. Click for the subscription from which you want to deassign users.
  5. Click for the users you want to deassign from the subscription. The users will be informed by email.

How can I continue to use an expired subscription?

If you subscribed to a service that can be used for a limited time only, your subscription automatically expires when this time has passed. If you want to continue to use such a service, you need to upgrade the subscription.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator, or as a subscription manager or OU administrator responsible for the subscription.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Subscriptions on the left side. As an organization administrator, you can see and manage all subscriptions of your organization. As a subscription manager, you can access your own subscriptions. As an OU administrator, you can access your own subscriptions and those of your organizational units.
  4. Click for the expired subscription you still want to use.
  5. Click the Up/Downgrade tab.
  6. Select the service to which you want to upgrade your subscription. The available services are defined by the supplier.

How can I terminate a subscription?

Terminating a subscription is only possible if no users are currently working with the underlying software. The subscription will be terminated immediately, and the service can no longer be used.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator, or as a subscription manager or OU administrator responsible for the subscription.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Subscriptions on the left side. As an organization administrator, you can see and manage all subscriptions of your organization. As a subscription manager, you can access your own subscriptions. As an OU administrator, you can access your own subscriptions and those of your organizational units.
  4. Click for the subscription you want to terminate.
  5. Click Terminate and confirm the termination.

You will be sent an email informing you about the termination. All users assigned to the subscription will also be informed by email.

I can't access the subscription I want to manage, what is wrong?

In order to manage a subscription, you need to be an organization administrator, a user with the subscription manager or OU administrator role who owns the subscription, or an OU administrator of the organizational unit to which the subscription is assigned. The initial owner of a subscription is the user who creates it.

If you can't access the subscription you want to manage, contact your organization administrator. He can assign the required rights and roles to other users as well as remove them. He can also set, change, and remove the owner of a subscription. Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Subscriptions on the left side.
  4. Click for the subscription you want to work on.
  5. Click the Configuration tab.
  6. Click next to Owner.
  7. Change the subscription owner as desired:

    • To set or change the owner, select the desired user. All users with administrative rights, the subscription manager role, or the OU administrator role are offered for selection.
    • To remove the owner, select This subscription does not have a dedicated owner. Only users with administrative rights can manage subscriptions without owners.
  8. Click OK and then Save on the Configuration tab.

How do I assign a subscription to an organizational unit?

When you subscribe to a service, you can assign the subscription to an organizational unit. As an organization administrator or an OU administrator of the unit in question, you can change this assignment later at any time.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator or an OU administrator responsible for the subscription.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Subscriptions on the left side.
  4. Click for the subscription you want to work on.
  5. Click the Configuration tab.
  6. Click next to Organizational unit.
  7. Change the organizational unit as desired:
    • To set or change the organizational unit, select the desired unit. As a users with administrative rights, you can select any unit of your organization. As an OU administrator, you can choose one of the units you are responsible for.
    • To remove the subscription from an organizational unit, select This subscription is not assigned to any organizational unit. This option is not available for OU administrators.
  8. Click OK and then Save on the Configuration tab.

Be aware of the following :

Changing the organizational unit for a subscription has no effect on the subscription owner or the users assigned to the subscription, even if they are not members of the new unit.

The organizational unit a subscription is assigned to appears in related invoices and reports. If you change the organizational unit for a subscription, only the new unit is included in the invoices and reports.

Payment

How do I specify payment information?

When you subscribe to a service that is not free of charge, you are prompted to specify how you are going to pay for it before you can confirm your subscription.

You have to specify a billing address and the payment type:

  • The billing address defines which organization name and address will be printed on your invoices and to which email address your invoices will be sent. If billing addresses have already been defined, you can select one of them. As an organization administrator, you can define a new billing address, if required.
  • The payment type determines how you want to pay for the subscription. If payment types have already been defined, you can select one of them. As an organization administrator, you can define a new payment type, if required.

You can change the billing addresses and payment types at any time.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Payment on the left side.
  4. To edit an existing billing address or payment type, click for it.
  5. To add a new billing address or payment type, click Add Billing Address or Add Payment Type.

When do I start getting charged?

As soon as you have subscribed to a service, you are charged for it according to the price model the service is based on.

As soon as you terminate a subscription, you are no longer charged for it.

Users and Units

How do I register other users?

If you register yourself, you are automatically assigned administrative rights. With these rights, you can register other users of your organization and assign them to subscriptions.

Here is how:

  1. Log in.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Users & Units.
  4. Click Add User on the Users tab.
  5. Enter the profile data for the new user and click Save.
  6. In the next dialog, check the desired roles. If you do not check any role, the user is registered as a standard user without any specific privileges.
  7. If desired, assign the user to existing organizational units and subscriptions by checking them. For each organizational unit you can choose the userâ€™s role.
  8. Click Save.

For each registered user, a user account is created and the user is notified by email of his registration. The email includes all the information required to log in. The new user is automatically added to your organization's default organizational unit. The default organizational unit contains all users currently registered for your organization. Click here for details.

If you are using a remote LDAP system to administrate users, you do not need to register them individually but import them from the LDAP system. Click here for details.

If you are not using a remote LDAP system to administrate users, you can also register multiple users in one operation by importing a user data file. Click here for details.

Be aware of the following:

If you change the role assignments of a user, the new assignments take effect with the user's next login.

How can I import users from a remote LDAP system?

If you are using an LDAP system to administrate users, and the access to this LDAP system has been enabled for you, you can import users instead of registering them manually.

Here is how:

  1. Log in.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Users & Units.
  4. Click Import Users on the Users tab.
  5. Identify the users you want to import.

    You can apply filter criteria to select specific users only. To list all users matching the filter criteria, click Search.

    You can use the following filter criteria:

    • Wildcard (*): All users existing in the LDAP system are listed. Be aware that a maximum of 100 users can be listed. Use more specific filter criteria, if necessary, for example, the starting character of user IDs together with a wildcard.
    • Characters and wildcards (*): All users are listed whose ID matches the specified characters. For example, if you enter *o*, all users whose ID contains the character o are listed.
    • User ID: To import one specific user, enter his ID directly.
  6. Select the check boxes for the users you want to import. If the email address of a user cannot be read from the LDAP system, you have to enter the email address manually.
  7. Assign the desired user roles. Users to whom you do not assign any role are registered as standard users without any specific privileges.
  8. To start the import, click Import.

The selected users are imported and registered. After a successful import, the users are sent an email with the URL for accessing the provided services. They are automatically added to your organization's default organizational unit. The default organizational unit contains all users currently registered for your organization. Click here for details.

If the user ID of a user to be imported already exists in the platform, the email address of this user (e.g. userSmith@organization.com) is used as user ID for logging in to the system. If yet another user with the same ID is imported, the new user ID is constructed using the existing ID followed by the "at" sign (@) and the ID of the organization the user belongs to (e.g. userSmith@abcdefg). The user is sent an email with the respective user ID.

User data maintained in the LDAP system, such as the password, first name, or last name, can only be changed in the LDAP system. If the data is changed, the marketplace automatically synchronizes it. Be aware that an automatic synchronization is not possible in case a user ID is changed in the LDAP system. The user has to be imported again.

How can I change the LDAP settings for my organization?

As an organization administrator, you can edit and change the LDAP settings for your organization.

Here is how:

  1. Log in.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Users & Units.
  4. Click Edit LDAP Settings on the Users tab.
  5. Click Export to store the current LDAP settings for your organization in a file on your file system.
  6. Check the settings in the file and change them as required.
  7. To load the changed settings, click the File name entry field or Browse, and select the file.
  8. Click Import. All existing LDAP settings are replaced by the ones in the file.
  9. Click Test connection to make sure that the connection to your LDAP system can be established.

The LDAP settings are immediately applied for your organization.

When you provide invalid LDAP settings, you and all users of your organization can no longer log in to the marketplace. In this case, contact a service seller and request the correction of your organization's LDAP settings.

Which keys must be defined in an LDAP configuration file?

The following keys must be defined in a configuration file for enabling access to an organization's LDAP system:

Key Description
LDAP_URL Mandatory. Provider URL of the LDAP server. This LDAP server is used for user authentication.

Example: LDAP_URL=ldap://myldapserver.lan.est.company.de:389
LDAP_BASE_DN Mandatory. Position in the LDAP directory tree at which to start looking for users.

Example: LDAP_BASE_DN=ou=people,dc=est,dc=mycompany,dc=de
LDAP_PRINCIPAL Optional. Name of the user who is allowed to query the LDAP server.

Example: LDAP_PRINCIPAL=uid=admin,ou=system
LDAP_CREDENTIALS Optional. Password of the user who is allowed to query the LDAP server.

Example: LDAP_CREDENTIALS=secret
LDAP_ATTR_UID Mandatory. LDAP attribute from which a user ID is read.

Example: LDAP_ATTR_UID=uid
LDAP_ATTR_EMAIL Optional. LDAP attribute from which the email address of a user is read.

Example: LDAP_ATTR_EMAIL=scalixEmailAddress
LDAP_ATTR_FIRST_NAME Optional. LDAP attribute from which the first name of a user is read.

Example: LDAP_ATTR_FIRST_NAME=givenName
LDAP_ATTR_LAST_NAME Optional. LDAP attribute from which the last name of a user is read.

Example: LDAP_ATTR_LAST_NAME=sn
LDAP_ATTR_ADDITIONAL_NAME Optional. LDAP attribute from which any additional name of a user is read.

Example: LDAP_ATTR_LAST_NAME=sn
LDAP_ATTR_LOCALE Optional. LDAP attribute from which the default language to be stored for a user is read.

Example: LDAP_ATTR_LOCALE=locale
LDAP_CONTEXT_FACTORY Mandatory. Context factory which provides the API to query the LDAP server.

Example: LDAP_CONTEXT_FACTORY=com.sun.jndi.ldap.LdapCtxFactory
LDAP_ATTR_REFERRAL Optional. Property defining how LDAP referrals are to be processed.

If an organization uses an Active Directory with sub-domains from which users are to be imported into the platform, the sub-domains can be modeled as referrals. In this sense, a referral is a reference to another directory partition or sub-domain. By default, values from referrals are not retrieved.

This property can take on the following values:
follow: Referrals are followed, i.e. users are imported from all referenced directory partitions or sub-domains.
ignore: Referrals are ignored (default), i.e. users are imported from the current domain directory only.
Example: LDAP_ATTR_REFERRAL=ignore

How can I import users from a file?

As an organization administrator, you can import users from a file instead of registering them manually. This is possible, if you are not using an LDAP system for user authentication.

Here is how:

  1. Create a file containing the required user data. Click here for details on the format and content of the file.
  2. Log in.
  3. Click Account in the top bar.
  4. Click Users & Units.
  5. Click Browse on the Users tab, and select the file containing the user data.
  6. Click Import.

    The system validates the user data file before starting the actual import. It checks whether:

    • All lines containing user data are complete (all fields are specified).
    • The spelling of the values for title and user roles is correct.
    • Multiple user roles are enclosed in double quotes.

    If an error is found during the validation of the user data file, a corresponding message is displayed and the import does not start. You must correct the information in the file before you can start the import again.

    You cannot start multiple imports at a time. You need to wait until the current import is finished. For long lists of users, this may take some time.


As soon as the import is finished, all users who have been imported and registered successfully are sent an email with the login data and an initial password to be used for the first login. The newly registered users are automatically added to your organization's default organizational unit. The default organizational unit contains all users currently registered for your organization. Click here for details.

You are sent an email with the following information:

  • Number of users who were imported and registered successfully.
  • For every user that was not imported: user ID and reason.

    Possible reasons are:

    • A user with the specified ID already exists.
    • The specified user ID is invalid.
    • The specified user role is not available for your organization. For example, if your organization is a supplier, you cannot specify the BROKER_MANAGER user role.

What is the format and content of a user data file?

As an organization administrator, you can import multiple users of your organization and register them with the platform. The user data must be provided in a file in csv (comma-separated values) format.

The following rules apply:

  • The data for one user is provided in one line. Empty lines are ignored.
  • The user data file is saved in UTF-8 encoding.
  • The user data is provided in the following sequence:

    1. User ID (mandatory)
    2. Email address (mandatory)
    3. Language (mandatory)
    4. Title (optional)
    5. First name (optional)
    6. Last name (optional)
    7. One or several user roles to be assigned to the imported user (mandatory if no marketplace is selected)
  • The user data fields are separated by a comma each. If an optional field does not contain any data, it must be empty and separated by a comma from the next field.

The fields can take on the following values:

Field Value(s)
User ID Mandatory. ID with which the user is to log in to the platform.

User IDs are restricted to 100 characters and must not contain any of the following characters:

! " # $ % & ' * + , / : ; < = > ? \ ^ `
Email address Mandatory. Email address of the user. It is used for notifying the user about the registration.

The system checks whether the syntax of the given email address is valid, and whether the domain name corresponds to the standards as defined and maintained by the Internet Assigned Numbers Authority (IANA).

Examples: user.name@domain.arpa, user.name@domain.org, user@mycompany.lan.uk
Language Mandatory. ISO code of the language in which the user will work by default, for example, en (English).
Title Optional. Salutation.

Allowed values: MR or MS

Observe that the value is case-sensitive and must be specified as indicated.
First name Optional.
Last name Optional.
User role Mandatory if no marketplace is selected at the user interface. If a marketplace is selected and no role is specified, a standard user without any privileges is registered.

The user roles that can be specified depend on the role of the organization for which the users are to be imported. Valid user roles are:

ORGANIZATION_ADMIN (administrator) for organizations with any role.

SUBSCRIPTION_MANAGER (subscription manager) for organizations with any role.

PLATFORM_OPERATOR (operator) for platform operator organizations.

MARKETPLACE_OWNER (marketplace manager) for marketplace owner organizations.

SERVICE_MANAGER (service manager) for supplier organizations.

TECHNOLOGY_MANAGER (technology manager) for technology provider organizations.

BROKER_MANAGER (broker manager) for broker organizations.

RESELLER_MANAGER (reseller manager) for reseller organizations.

Observe the following:

  • The role names are case-sensitive and must be specified as indicated above.
  • If several user roles are specified, they must be separated by a comma and enclosed in double quotes (Example: "ORGANIZATION_ADMIN, RESELLER_MANAGER"). Blanks between role names and fields are ignored.
  • If one user role is specified that is not available for the organization for which to import users, it is ignored and the affected users are not registered.
  • If several roles are specified for a user, and at least one of these roles is valid for the organization, the user is imported and registered successfully, invalid roles are ignored.

Sample user data file:

user1,user1@company.com,en,MS,Jane,Smith,ORGANIZATION_ADMIN
user2,user2@company.com,de,MR,John F.,Cool,SUBSCRIPTION_MANAGER
user3,user3@company.com,en,,,,ORGANIZATION_ADMIN
user4,user4@company.com,en,,,Admin,"SERVICE_MANAGER,ORGANIZATION_ADMIN"
user5,user5@company.com,en,MR,,Mueller-Siegel,SERVICE_MANAGER
user6,user6@company.com,en,MR,Joe,StandardUser,

What is an organizational unit?

An organizational unit is a set of users within an organization. It can, for example, represent a department in a company, an individual project, or a single person.

An organizational unit provides a means to restrict the visibility of services to the unit members. When a user logs in on a marketplace, he has access only to the services that are visible to the organizational units to which he belongs. An organizational unit can also be assigned one or more subscriptions, for example, for cost controlling purposes. Note, however, that users can be assigned to and work with a subscription independently of the organizational units they belong to.

As an organization administrator, you are responsible for creating organizational units. A user can be a member of multiple units. Each organization can have any number of units.

As an organization administrator, you can change the properties and members of any unit in your organization. One or more users in a unit can be appointed as OU administrators. For their units, OU administrators can change the description and members, define the visible services, and create, modify, and terminate subscriptions.

Assign users to organizational units carefully. Take into account that, for example, a service manager who publishes a service to a marketplace will not be able to see the service on the marketplace if you do not assign him to the appropriate organizational unit.

What is the default organizational unit?

Each organization has a default organizational unit to which all users of the organization belong.

Any service that is published to a marketplace is automatically visible to the members of the default unit. As an organization administrator, you have to explicitly mark a service as not visible for the unit, if required.

As an organization administrator, you are also responsible for creating additional organizational units. As soon as you assign a user to one or more additional units, the services marked as visible to the members of the default unit are no longer relevant for this user.

It is not possible to add or remove users from the default organizational unit. However, the name of the default organizational unit can be changed, and services can be assigned and deassigned.

Be aware of the following:

As an organization administrator, OU administrator, or subscription manager, you can upgrade or downgrade subscriptions. If you do so, make sure that all services that are involved in an up/downgrade path need to be made accessible in the same organizational unit. The available services are defined by the supplier.

How do I create and manage organizational units?

As an organization administrator you can create, edit and delete organizational units. OU administrators can change the descriptions and members of the organizational units they are responsible for.

Here is how to create an organizational unit:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Users & Units or Organizational units on the left side.
  4. On the Organizational units tab, click Add Unit.
  5. Enter an appropriate name, reference ID and description.
  6. Click Save to create the organizational unit.
  7. In the following dialog, check the services that are to be visible for the members of the organizational unit.
  8. To assign users to the new organizational unit check them in the Organizational unit members table and select their roles as required.
  9. Click Save.

Here is how to edit organizational units:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator or as an OU administrator.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Users & Units on the left side.
  4. On the Organizational units tab, click next to the name of the organizational unit you want to edit.
  5. Make the desired changes in the Edit organizational unit dialog:
    • To change the unit name, enter the new name in the Name field. This action is only available to organization administrators.
    • To change the Reference ID, remove the old ID and enter a new one. This action is only available to organization administrators.
    • To make services visible to the unit, check them under Accessible services.
    • To make a service invisible, deselect it under Accessible services.
    • To add new members to the unit, check them in the table Organizational unit members. As an organization administrator, you can set or remove the role of an OU administrator for each user in the Role in unit column.
    • To remove members from the unit, deselect them in the table Organizational unit members.
  6. Click Save

Here is how to delete an organizational unit:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Users & Units on the left side.
  4. On the Organizational units tab, click next to the unit you would like to delete.

Users are notified by e-mail when they are added to or removed from an organizational unit, or if the name of a unit they are assigned to is changed.

Be aware of the following:

As an organization administrator, OU administrator or subscription manager, you can upgrade or downgrade subscriptions. If you do so, make sure that all services that are involved in an up/downgrade path are accessible in the same organizational unit. The available services are defined by the supplier.

If you change the role assignments of a user, the new assignments take effect with the user's next login.

Processes

How can I carry out actions under process control?

Certain actions on the marketplace can be carried out under the control of an external process control system. You can configure so-called triggers which are invoked when the actions are carried out. The triggers start the corresponding process in the process control system. If approval for an action is required, the action is suspended until it is approved or rejected in the process control system. If no approval is required, the action is instantly executed.

As a prerequisite for controlling actions by processes, a notification service must exist and be deployed. This service forms the interface between the marketplace and the process control system. For details on implementing such a service, contact the marketplace owner.

Then you can define the process triggers.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Processes on the left side.
  4. Click Add Process Trigger, and proceed with the definition of the trigger details.
  5. From the Type list, select the action which is to be subject to process control. The list contains all actions for which triggers can be configured:

    • A subscription is to be added (Subscribe to service).
    • A subscription is to be terminated (Terminate subscription).
    • A subscription is to be changed, for example, renamed (Modify subscription).
    • A subscription is to be upgraded or downgraded (Up/Downgrade subscription).
    • A user is to be assigned to or removed from a subscription (Assign users to subscription).
      For the case that a user is automatically assigned to a new subscription when subscribing to a service, no trigger can be configured. The Assign users to subscription trigger only applies to users that are manually assigned to a subscription.
    • A billing run is completed, and the billing data for a billing period is calculated (Billing run finished).
    • A user is to be registered (Register user).
      Triggers can only be configured for registering single users. The import of multiple users in one operation from an LDAP system or a user data file cannot be executed under process control.

    As a supplier, you can configure the following additional triggers:

    • A customer is to be registered (Register customer).
    • The payment types for a customer are to be changed (Manage payment types for customer).
    • A marketable service is to be activated (Activate service).
    • A marketable service is to be deactivated (Deactivate service).
    • A user subscribes to your service (Subscription created (any user)).
    • A user modifies a subscription to your service (Subscription modified (any user)).
    • A user terminates a subscription to your service (Subscription terminated (any user)).

  6. In the Target type field, stay with the entry Web Service. Currently, this is the only supported type of target.
  7. In the Target URL field, enter the URL of the WSDL file of the notification service implemented for your organization.
  8. Specify whether the execution of the action is to be suspended until it is approved or rejected in the external process control system.
    If not, the action is instantly executed. The process control system is informed if the action for the non-suspending trigger is executed successfully.
    Note that for the following triggers for suppliers the execution of the action cannot be suspended: Subscription created (any user), Subscription modified (any user), and Subscription terminated (any user).
  9. Click Save when you are finished.

How can I update or delete process triggers?

You can easily update or delete process triggers you have defined.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Processes on the left side.
  4. Select the trigger you want to update or delete in the list.
  5. Proceed with editing or deleting the trigger.
    If the trigger to be edited has already started corresponding processes in the process control system, you can only change the name for the trigger. You have to abort the corresponding processes before you can edit the other elements of the trigger definition.
  6. If you have updated the trigger, click Save when you are finished.
    If you want to delete the trigger, confirm the deletion.

How can I manage my processes?

When an action that is subject to process control is executed, you can view its current status. The following statuses are possible:

  • Started: The action was initialized on the platform.
  • Waiting for reply: The action has to be approved in the process control system before it is executed.
  • Approved: The action has been approved in the process control system, and it has been executed. For example, if you requested to subscribe to a service, the subscription has been created.
  • Rejected: The action has not been approved in the process control system; it will not be executed.
  • Notified: The action has been executed successfully, and the process control system has been informed about its execution.
  • The external system did not accept the action: The communication with the process control system failed; the action is not executed.
  • The platform could not execute the action: An error occurred in the execution of the action after the action had been approved in the process control system. The action is not executed.
  • Aborted: The action has been aborted.

In addition to viewing the current status of your actions, you can also abort or delete process-controlled actions.

Here is how:

  1. Log in as an organization administrator or as a subscription manager.
  2. Click Account in the top bar.
  3. Click Processes on the left side.

If you are logged in as a subscription manager, you can view all your own actions which are subject to process control. The Description column displays the action that was requested. The Status column shows its current status.
If you are logged in as an administrator, you can choose whether all actions of your organization's users which have triggered a process are to be listed, or only your own actions.

To abort the execution of an action, select the check box next to it and click Abort. Only actions in the Waiting for reply status can be aborted.
The status is changed to Aborted. The action no longer waits for approval in the process control system. It is not executed and can be deleted from the list. The process control system is informed about the abortion of the action and can stop processing the request for approval.

To delete an action from the list, select the check box next to it and click Delete. Only actions that are not in the Waiting for reply status can be deleted.

Service Operations

How can I execute a service operation?

Services may support special operations, such as backup, shutdown, or startup of a specific application, depending on the implementation of the underlying software. If defined for a service, you can carry out such operations from a subscription.

Here is how:

  1. Click My Subscriptions in the top bar.
  2. Find the subscription you want to use.
  3. In the Details section, from the subscription's drop-down list, select the operation you want to execute. A detailed explanation of the operation is displayed. The list contains all operations that are defined for the service underlying your subscription.
  4. Click Execute.

Depending on how the operation is defined in the underlying service, the result of the operation is displayed instantly after its execution, or the status of execution is displayed (for asynchronous, long-running operations).

How can I view the status of service operations?

You can view the execution status of all operations you initiated for subscriptions to which you are assigned.

As an administrator, you can view the execution status of the operations for all subscriptions of your organization, including the ones you initiated as a user assigned to a subscription.

As a subscription manager, you can view the execution status of the operations for the subscriptions you own, including the ones you executed as user assigned to a subscription. As an OU administrator, you can view the execution status of the operations for the subscriptions assigned to your organizational unit.

Here is how:

  1. Click Account in the top bar.
  2. Click Operations on the left side.

    The list of operations contains information on

    • the name of the operation
    • the subscription the operation was exectuted for
    • who executed the operation
    • the execution date
    • the current status of execution.

      An operation can either be in the Running (not yet fully executed), Completed (fully executed), or Error state. In case of an error, the reason for it is shown.

You can remove entries from the list of operations by selecting them and clicking Delete. Removing an entry does not affect the execution of the operation.
